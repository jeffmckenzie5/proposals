Pain points

Two problems. One platform without the tools to fix either.

You flagged two operational gaps on your survey. Here's what's actually causing them — and what they're costing you in staff time each year.

Pain Point 01
Staff spends too much time on manual logging and lookup
Quadient WTS is a Generation 1 system. That means every package requires a staff member to scan a carrier barcode, manually key recipient information, and select the correct person from a list. The system records an event — it does not make an assignment. That decision still belongs to your staff, on every single item, every day.

The math is straightforward. At the industry average of 22.5 seconds per item for Gen 1 processing, 13,000 annual packages translates to roughly 81 hours of intake labor per year just on packages. Letter mail, processed manually without any system, adds time that simply isn't measured — because no record is being kept.
81 hrs/yr on package intake alone at current processing speed
Pain Point 02
Student mailbox management is inefficient or outdated
Managing individual student PO Boxes without a digital accountability layer means your mailroom team owns the entire process manually: sorting, distributing, and fielding questions about what arrived, whether it was picked up, and where something went if a student claims they never got it.

The core problem is the absence of an image at intake. Without a photo record tied to each piece at the moment it enters the system, there is no way to establish chain of custody below the mailroom level. When a student asks about a piece of mail they never received, the answer is either "we don't know" or an investigation that costs staff time with no conclusive evidence either way.

Students currently have no visibility into what's waiting in their box until they physically check it. That creates unnecessary trips, buildup in boxes, and recurring inquiries about expected mail.
No image-at-intake = no chain of custody for 13,260 letter mail pieces/year

How it works
One image handles what every other system still makes your staff do.

Every package and every piece of letter mail that comes through your door already has the information you need — it's printed on the label or the envelope. The problem has always been getting that information out without someone standing there reading it, typing it, and deciding on every item. Every other system still makes your staff do that work.

1
Cognitive OCR
A staff member photographs the item — package or letter mail. That single image triggers everything. Cognitive OCR reads the label or envelope the way an expert would: damaged labels, handwriting, truncated names, non-standard fonts. It extracts structured recipient data without human intervention. For your WTS operation, this replaces the scan-type-select sequence entirely.
Package auto-assignment — Cognitive OCR reads the label and Intelligent Assignment identifies the recipient automatically
Letter mail — IMB barcode recognition and auto-assignment from the native envelope data, no label required
2
Intelligent Assignment
Cognitive OCR feeds directly into Intelligent Assignment, which identifies the correct recipient automatically and routes the item to the correct location. No typing. No lookups. No repeat decisions on the same name variation. When staff selects the correct recipient for an ambiguous label, the system creates a permanent association — it learns. The longer it runs, the less your staff intervenes. 90%+ automatic assignment rate reported by current higher education clients, versus 50–60% with typical Gen 1 systems.
3
Image-Based Notification
The moment an item is imaged at intake, the recipient receives a photo of their actual item — package or letter — along with where it's waiting for pickup. This is not a separate notification step; the image already exists because imaging is the intake process. Students with PO Boxes know what's in their box before they make the trip. Faculty and staff know their mail arrived without calling the mailroom. Current clients report 60–80% reduction in inbound inquiry volume as a direct result.
Recipient notification — the student receives a photo of their item the moment it is imaged at intake
4
Native Mail Processing
Letter mail is processed from the native data on the envelope. No system-generated barcode is created. No label is affixed. The USPS Intelligent Mail Barcode is captured natively, making every piece uniquely identifiable without adding any physical step to the workflow. This is the clearest proof that Received Digital was built for mail, not adapted to it. For Aurora's PO Box operation, this means every piece of student and faculty letter mail gets a chain of custody record without changing how it physically moves through your center.
5
Immutable Chain of Custody
Every item, every event, every staff action is timestamped from the moment of first image in a record that cannot be manually altered. Not a log the mailroom maintains — a record the system builds automatically on every single item. For packages and for letter mail equally. When a student disputes receipt of financial aid correspondence, a certified letter, or any other time-sensitive piece, the answer is an image and a timestamp, not a staff recollection.
Batch review — packages processed in sequence, each automatically assigned and ready for notification
Batch review — letter mail processed the same way, from native envelope data, with no labels or additional steps
